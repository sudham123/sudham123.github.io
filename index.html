What are exoplanets?

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside our solar system. Thousands have been discovered using methods like the transit method, where a planet passes in front of its star, dimming the light we see. These planets vary widely in size, composition, and temperature. Furthermore some resemble Earth, while others are gas giants like Neptune. Studying exoplanets helps scientists understand planetary formation and the potential for life beyond Earth. Missions like Kepler and TESS have significantly advanced our knowledge of these distant worlds.
